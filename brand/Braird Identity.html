01 First Shoots fresh, botanical, contemporary

The most literal read of the name. Both R's literally sprout — a young leaf grows from the arm of each one — so the meaning (first shoots after sowing) and the disambiguation device become the same gesture. Clean, optimistic, grounded by a deep-forest ink so it reads considered, never twee.

02 Resurface restrained, intelligent, near-monochrome

The most durable and "app-like" route. A quiet ink-on-paper serif with one accent that does a single job: it lights the two R's and draws a fine arc that surfaces from below to connect them — the verbal identity, resurface and connect, made literal. Reads as a tool you'll keep for a decade, not a launch.

03 Take Root warm, literary, earthy

The warmest, most bookish route — a hand-bound, well-worn register where a garden meets a library. The two R's are terracotta, the colour of sown earth; the logomark is a single seed splitting into two shoots with roots beneath. Resurface. Reconnect. Take root. Old-world without being twee, thanks to the literary serif and grounded clay.

Recommendation

Lead with 02 · Resurface; keep 01 in reserve.

Resurface best fits a private, durable desktop tool for contemplative knowledge workers — the near-monochrome restraint ages well, the connect-arc carries the product's whole promise, and a single accent is the cleanest possible answer to the two-R problem at favicon size. First Shoots is the strongest if the brand wants to feel more alive and approachable up front. Take Root is the move if the product leans hard into the literary, archival register.
